Abstract—The rapid expansion of 6G edge computing intro-
duces critical challenges in federated orchestration: massive de-
vice density, heterogeneous workloads, and strict latency require-
ments. Existing federated reinforcement learning approaches lack
semantic awareness and suffer from communication inefﬁciency
and convergence instability at scale, resulting in elevated latency,
excessive power consumption, and degraded service quality.
We propose FedSemGNN, a semantic-aware hierarchical fed-
erated reinforcement learning framework that integrates graph
neural networks with Proximal Policy Optimization for edge
service orchestration. The framework employs a custom continual
learning encoder to extract 16-dimensional semantic embeddings
from service characteristics including resource demands, service
types, and temporal patterns. A two-layer graph convolutional
network captures network topology and resource constraints,
while the hierarchical aggregation operates at two levels: local
synchronization every 10 epochs and global synchronization
every 50 epochs. The lightweight actor-critic architecture with
16 hidden units enables ultra-low orchestration latency.
We evaluate FedSemGNN in EdgeSimPy using a heterogeneous
edge infrastructure with mobile users following realistic mobility
patterns over 1,000 timesteps. Scalability is validated through
simulated experiments across two deployment scales: 100 nodes
(IoT edge) and 10,000 nodes (enterprise edge), representing
a 100 × scale range with 0.967 × scaling factor demonstrating
sub-linear computational complexity. FedSemGNN is compared
against four baselines: FlatFedPPO, HierFedPPO, HSQF, and
RandomPlacement. Results demonstrate substantial improve-
ments: normalized reward of 0.91 (8.4 percent higher than
FlatFedPPO), orchestration latency of 0.309–0.328 ms across
all simulated scales (315 times faster than FlatFedPPO), power
consumption of 72.1 W (36.2 times lower), and communication ef-
ﬁciency of 1.394 reward per MB (174 times higher). FedSemGNN
achieves 100 percent semantic ﬁdelity with zero migrations com-
pared to 6,840 in FlatFedPPO. These simulation results establish
FedSemGNN as an exceptionally scalable orchestration solution
with sub-linear computational scaling for next-generation edge
intelligence.

I. I NTRODUCTION
The imminent arrival of 6G networks promises to enable
next-generation edge intelligence with ultra-low latency, high
reliability, and context-aware services including extended real-
ity, autonomous systems, and tactile internet applications [ 1].
These use cases demand ﬁne-grained orchestration of com-
puting and data services across heterogeneous edge nodes [ 2].
Traditional cloud-based approaches cannot meet such require-
ments due to latency bottlenecks, reinforcing the importance
of edge computing to bring processing closer to end users [ 3].
The dynamic and resource-constrained nature of edge en-
vironments combined with energy constraints, privacy re-
quirements, and workload variability introduces substantial
orchestration challenges [ 4]. Recent studies have investigated
reinforcement learning for adaptive orchestration, allowing
real-time learning of placement and migration strategies [ 2].
However, centralized RL methods suffer from scalability lim-
itations and cannot preserve user privacy. Furthermore, most
RL-based orchestration approaches disregard semantic content
in service tasks, leading to suboptimal placements in latency-
sensitive or context-dependent scenarios such as extended
reality or real-time analytics [ 5].
Federated reinforcement learning provides a promising al-
ternative by distributing policy learning across edge nodes
without sharing raw data [ 6]. While FRL frameworks have
shown promise in handling privacy and scalability, they
frequently overlook two critical dimensions: semantic task
awareness and network topology encoding [ 7], [ 8]. Modern
edge workloads often include semantic information such as
task intent or content category, which if properly modeled can
signiﬁcantly enhance placement ﬁdelity and decision stabil-
ity [ 5]. At the same time, orchestration effectiveness depends
on network structure, including link quality, proximity, and
cluster connectivity. Y et most FRL methods treat edge nodes
as unstructured agents without considering topological rela-

II. R ELATED WORK
A. Federated Reinforcement Learning for Edge Orchestration
Federated reinforcement learning (FRL) has emerged as a
robust approach for edge orchestration under privacy con-
straints. Unlike centralized RL, FRL enables distributed agents
to train local policies without exposing raw data, which is
critical for 6G-enabled services [ 11], [ 12]. Recent studies
explore FRL in hybrid cloud-edge environments [ 13], [14] and
under hierarchical model training setups [ 15], with applica-
tions including anomaly detection, task ofﬂoading, and digital
twin optimization [ 16], [ 17]. Despite growing interest, most
current methods still rely on abstract or ﬂat feature vectors
and struggle to encode spatial or semantic structure, limiting
coordination under dynamic network topologies [ 18], [ 19].
B. Semantic-Aware Scheduling and Service Placement
Semantic embeddings offer robust generalization across or-
chestration tasks by encoding high-level intent and contextual
similarity. Early works have applied semantic modeling in
edge caching and service personalization [ 17], [ 20]. Seman-
tic compression has also been explored for communication-
aware model training [ 13], but integration into reinforcement
learning pipelines for distributed scheduling remains sparse.
Recent advances suggest that semantic representations can
reduce service migrations and enhance policy transfer across
domains [ 5], [ 21]. Our framework builds on this insight by
coupling semantic representations with federated PPO for
personalized orchestration policies.
C. GNN-Based Topology Encoding for Edge Intelligence
Graph neural networks (GNNs) are increasingly used to cap-
ture spatial relationships in wireless and edge environments.
They support topology-aware orchestration by encoding node
states, link quality, and global dependencies [ 8], [ 22]. GNN-
RL combinations have shown promise in multi-hop ofﬂoading,
edge caching, and vehicular scheduling [ 9], [ 23], though
few operate under federated constraints. Additionally, feder-
ated orchestration often lacks topology adaptation, limiting
robustness under dynamic infrastructure changes. Our work
integrates GNN-based topology modeling into a federated
PPO loop, enabling communication-efﬁcient and topology-
consistent policy learning.
D. Hierarchical Federated Learning for Communication Efﬁ-
ciency
Hierarchical federated learning (HFL) reduces synchroniza-
tion overhead by grouping clients into subregions that perform
local model updates before periodic global aggregation. This
model is especially suited for edge and IoT systems, where
communication cost and energy efﬁciency are paramount and
where clients exhibit heterogeneous energy, compute, and mo-
bility characteristics [ 24], [25]. While HFL has shown promise
in supervised learning, its use in reinforcement learning for
real-time orchestration remains limited [ 20]. Existing methods
often neglect dynamic service behaviors or lack mechanisms
for topology-informed coordination. In our work, we extend
HFL principles to federated PPO and apply it in a two-tier
control structure with local synchronization every 10 epochs
and global synchronization every 50 epochs. This results in
signiﬁcant communication savings, reducing total communi-
cation volume to 0.65 MB over 1,000 orchestration steps
compared to 105.0 MB for ﬂat federated PPO, representing
a 161 times reduction in communication overhead.
E. Deep RL-Based Edge Ofﬂoading and Resource Allocation
Recent work on deep reinforcement learning for edge com-
puting has demonstrated signiﬁcant advances in task ofﬂoading
and resource allocation. ECO-SDIoT [ 26] employs Double
DQN with software-deﬁned networking for global-view op-
timization, achieving 20–60 ms task ofﬂoading latency in IoT
environments. While effective for batch-oriented ofﬂoading,
ECO-SDIoT lacks semantic awareness and operates without
federated privacy guarantees. GFL-LFF [ 27] combines GNN
with federated learning for privacy-preserving data aggregation
in Industrial IoT, reporting 1.6 s latency for secure data
sharing. Although GFL-LFF demonstrates the beneﬁts of GNN
integration with federated approaches, it focuses on data
aggregation rather than real-time orchestration. FRPVC [ 28]
applies federated deep reinforcement learning with DDQN
for cost-efﬁcient video caching in energy-constrained mobile
edge networks, optimizing cache hit rates through denoised
autoencoders and MDP-based resource allocation.
While these methods demonstrate effective deep RL opti-
mization for speciﬁc edge applications, they address narrow
use cases (ofﬂoading, data aggregation, caching) without gen-
eralizing to semantic-aware task placement. Moreover, value-
based methods (Double DQN, DDQN) can suffer from insta-
bility in high-dimensional action spaces compared to policy
gradient approaches. FedSemGNN advances beyond these
works by: (1) incorporating explicit semantic embeddings
for application-aware orchestration, (2) employing hierarchical
PPO for more stable policy learning than value-based methods,
(3) using GNN topology encoding for efﬁcient network repre-
sentation, and (4) achieving sub-millisecond latency (0.36 ms)
representing 55 to 4,444 times improvements over state-of-the-
art methods while maintaining federated privacy guarantees.
F . Positioning of Our Work
To the best of our knowledge, no existing system jointly
integrates semantic embeddings, GNN-based topology encod-
ing, and hierarchical federated reinforcement learning within a
uniﬁed orchestration framework. Our proposed FedSemGNN
is the ﬁrst to combine these dimensions in a federated setting,
implemented on the EdgeSimPy simulator [ 10]. The system
supports detailed instrumentation across reward, latency, com-
munication, power, and ﬁdelity metrics. Evaluated over 1,000
orchestration steps on a heterogeneous edge infrastructure
with scalability validated across 100 to 10,000 nodes (100 ×
scale range) through simulated experiments, FedSemGNN

III. S YSTEM ARCHITECTURE
FedSemGNN enables scalable, low-latency, and privacy-
preserving orchestration for 6G edge networks by integrating
three core components:
1) Semantic-Aware Task Representation : Custom contin-
ual learning encoder extracts compact semantic embed-
dings from service characteristics to enable intent-aware
orchestration [ 17], [ 18], [ 21],
2) GNN-Based Topology Encoding : Graph convolutional
network captures spatial dependencies and inter-node
relationships across the edge infrastructure [ 9], [ 20], and
3) Hierarchical Federated PPO : Two-level synchroniza-
tion coordinates distributed policy learning while pre-
serving privacy and minimizing communication over-
head [ 11], [ 18], [ 19].
The architecture follows a hierarchical federated learn-
ing paradigm where multiple edge clusters operate semi-
autonomously while maintaining global coordination through
periodic aggregation. Each cluster contains resource monitors,
edge nodes with local orchestrators, and graph-based topol-
ogy encoders. User devices equipped with semantic encoders
generate service requests that are processed through the or-
chestration pipeline.
Local orchestrators make placement decisions using policies
learned through proximal policy optimization, leveraging both
semantic compatibility and topological context. The two-level
synchronization structureintra-cluster coordination and global
aggregationbalances responsiveness with communication efﬁ-
ciency, enabling the system to scale to large simulated edge
infrastructures while maintaining orchestration quality.
A. Semantic-Aware Task Representation
Service requests entering the FedSemGNN system are trans-
formed into compact semantic embeddings that capture task
intent and requirements. The semantic encoder processes het-
erogeneous service characteristics including resource demands
(CPU, memory, network bandwidth), functional service types
(web services, data analytics, real-time processing), temporal
patterns (time-of-day preferences, periodic workloads), and
quality-of-service requirements (latency sensitivity, throughput
constraints).
The encoder employs continual learning mechanisms to
adapt to evolving service landscapes without forgetting pre-
viously learned patterns [ 17], [ 18]. When new service types
emerge, the system preserves knowledge of existing services
while incorporating novel patterns through regularization and
experience replay. This adaptive capability is essential for
dynamic edge environments where application requirements
continuously evolve.
Each edge node maintains a semantic capability proﬁle that
reﬂects its functional specialization and historical workload
patterns. Semantic matching identiﬁes compatible task-server
pairs, enabling the system to maintain high placement ﬁdelity
by aligning service requirements with node capabilities [ 21],
[22]. Tasks are considered semantically compatible with nodes
when their embedding similarity exceeds a predeﬁned thresh-
old, ensuring quality-of-service consistency.
Figure 2 illustrates the complete semantic processing
pipeline from service arrival to placement decision. The
pipeline begins with service/task input and graph/context fea-
tures, which are processed by the semantic encoder to generate
initial embeddings. These embeddings are then reﬁned by
the GNN encoder to incorporate topological context, followed
by a projection head that produces the ﬁnal 16-dimensional
semantic vector z. The semantic vector undergoes similarity
search against server capability proﬁles to identify compati-
ble placement candidates, culminating in the ﬁnal placement
decision that selects both the target cluster and speciﬁc edge
node. A critical feedback loop (shown in purple dotted line)
enables semantic adaptation: performance signals and policy
outcomes are fed back to the semantic encoder, allowing the
system to continuously reﬁne its embedding space based on
actual orchestration results. This adaptive mechanism is key
to FedSemGNN’s ability to maintain perfect semantic ﬁdelity
while adapting to evolving workload patterns.
B. Topology Encoding with Graph Neural Networks
The edge computing infrastructure is modeled as a graph
where nodes represent compute servers and edges capture
communication links [ 9], [ 20] with associated costs (latency,
bandwidth, reliability). This graph representation enables the
system to reason about spatial relationships, network proxim-
ity, and resource distribution across the infrastructure.
Graph neural networks process this topological structure
through iterative neighborhood aggregation, where each node
accumulates information from its local neighborhood to build
progressively richer representations [ 9], [ 12]. Initial node
features include semantic afﬁnity scores, current resource
availability (CPU, memory, bandwidth), and historical usage
patterns. Through multiple aggregation layers, nodes develop
embeddings that encode both their local state and broader
network context from multi-hop neighborhoods.
The topology-aware embeddings enable spatially coherent
orchestration decisions. By accounting for network proxim-
ity and communication costs, the system can place related

C. Hierarchical Federated Proximal Policy Optimization
The orchestration framework employs hierarchical federated
learning to coordinate distributed policy learning across edge
clusters. Each local orchestrator maintains its own policy for
placement decisions, learning from local experiences while
periodically synchronizing with neighboring orchestrators and
the global coordinator.
The hierarchical structure operates at two levels: intra-
cluster coordination maintains coherence among orchestrators
within the same edge cluster, while global aggregation ensures
consistency across all clusters. This two-level design reduces
communication overhead compared to ﬂat federated architec-
tures where all agents must synchronize with a central server
for every update [ 18], [ 19]. Local coordination enables rapid
adaptation to cluster-speciﬁc workload patterns, while global
aggregation prevents policy divergence and ensures system-
wide optimization.
Privacy preservation is achieved through secure aggregation
protocols where only model parameters (not raw data or tra-
jectories) are transmitted during synchronization. Differential
privacy mechanisms add calibrated noise during aggregation to
provide formal privacy guarantees. Gradient clipping prevents
any single agent from dominating the aggregated policy,
ensuring fairness and stability [ 21], [ 29].
The policy learning algorithm employs proximal policy
optimization, which constrains policy updates to prevent de-
structively large changes that could destabilize training [ 11],
[30]. Each orchestrator maintains an actor-critic architecture:
the actor network selects placement actions based on current
state observations, while the critic network estimates state
values to guide policy improvement. The compact neural
architecture enables low-latency decision-making suitable for
real-time orchestration requirements.

E. Design Rationale and System Characteristics
The FedSemGNN architecture is designed to address three
fundamental challenges in 6G edge orchestration: semantic
heterogeneity of service requests, dynamic network topology,
and distributed coordination at scale.
Semantic Awareness: Traditional resource-based orchestra-
tion treats all services homogeneously, considering only CPU,
memory, and bandwidth requirements. FedSemGNN’s seman-
tic layer enables intent-aware placement by matching service
characteristics with node capabilities, improving quality-of-
service consistency and reducing placement violations [ 26],
[27].
Topology Encoding: Graph neural networks provide a
principled approach to encode network structure, enabling the
system to reason about spatial relationships and communi-
cation costs. This topological awareness reduces unnecessary
service migrations by accounting for network proximity when
making placement decisions.
Hierarchical Federation: The two-level synchronization
structure addresses the scalability-consistency tradeoff inher-
ent in distributed learning. Frequent local coordination main-
tains cluster coherence, while periodic global aggregation
ensures system-wide optimization without excessive commu-
nication overhead.
Compact Architecture: The neural components employ
small network architectures (16-64 hidden units) to enable
sub-millisecond decision latency. This design choice priori-
tizes inference speed over model capacity, recognizing that
orchestration decisions must be made in real-time as service
requests arrive.
Privacy Preservation: Secure aggregation and differential
privacy mechanisms ensure that no agent can infer information
about other agents’ local workloads or placement decisions.
This privacy guarantee would be essential for multi-tenant
edge infrastructures where competing service providers share
infrastructure.
The combination of these design choices enables Fed-
SemGNN to deliver scalable, low-latency orchestration while
maintaining semantic ﬁdelity, privacy, and energy efﬁciency.

A. System Model
We consider an edge computing network composed of N
heterogeneous edge servers interconnected by communication
links with varying latency and bandwidth. Each server v ∈ V
is deﬁned by a dynamic state vector xv(t) that includes:

• CPU utilization and available processing capacity,
• Memory availability and active allocation,
• Uplink and downlink bandwidth,
• Historical workload statistics,
• Semantic service afﬁnity scores.
The edge fabric is modeled as a weighted graph G = (V, E)
where edge weights encode communication costs (latency and
bandwidth). To improve scalability and reduce synchronization
overhead, the network employs hierarchical federated learn-
ing with two-level aggregation. Local orchestrators perform
task placement before participating in periodic global policy
aggregation, adapting dynamically to network topology and
workload patterns [ 31]–[33].

J. Summary of Key Findings
FedSemGNN achieves exceptional multi-dimensional per-
formance: (1) highest reward (0.91) through joint power-
latency optimization, (2) ultra-low latency (0.36 ms) enabling
real-time 6G operation, (3) perfect semantic ﬁdelity (100%)
with zero migrations, (4) lowest power (72.1 W) and com-

establish FedSemGNN as a strong foundation for semantic-
aware, hierarchical orchestration in next-generation edge en-

VI. C ONCLUSION
This paper introduced FedSemGNN, a hierarchical feder-
ated reinforcement learning framework for semantic-aware 6G
edge orchestration. By integrating 16-dimensional semantic
embeddings with continual learning, two-layer GCN topology
encoding, and hierarchical PPO with dual-level aggregation
(K1 = 10 , K2 = 50 epochs), FedSemGNN achieves sub-
linear computational complexity with 0.967 × scaling factor,
maintaining near-constant latency (0.309–0.328 ms) across
100 to 10,000 simulated nodes.
Evaluated in EdgeSimPy over 1,000 orchestration steps
with heterogeneous infrastructure and mobile users, Fed-
SemGNN demonstrates: normalized reward 0.91 (8.4% higher
than FlatFedPPO), orchestration latency 0.36 ms (315 × faster
than FlatFedPPO), perfect semantic ﬁdelity (100%) with zero
migrations, power consumption 72.1 W (36.2 × lower than
FlatFedPPO), and communication efﬁciency 1.394 reward/MB
(174× higher than FlatFedPPO). Comparisons with state-of-
the-art methods show 55–166 × latency improvement over
ECO-SDIoT and 4,444 × over GFL-LFF, with 76 × lower
communication overhead.
Four key contributions enable these results: (1) semantic
embeddings achieve 100% ﬁdelity and eliminate migrations
through intent-aware placement, (2) GCN topology encoding
captures multi-hop dependencies for spatially coherent de-
cisions, (3) hierarchical aggregation reduces communication
by 161 × while maintaining convergence, and (4) compact
neural architecture (16 hidden units) enables sub-millisecond
inference. The reward function Rt = max(0 , 10,000 − (Pt +
0.01 · Lt)) with smoothing α = 0.2 effectively balances power
and latency optimization while implicitly reinforcing semantic
alignment.
FedSemGNN establishes a validated foundation for privacy-
preserving orchestration in large-scale edge networks. Its
integration of semantic reasoning, graph-based spatial mod-
eling, and hierarchical coordination demonstrates sub-linear
computational complexity across a 100 × scale range (100–
10,000 nodes), addressing fundamental challenges of latency,
scalability, and energy efﬁciency for real-world 6G edge
deployments.
VII. L IMITATIONS AND FUTURE WORK
While FedSemGNN demonstrates strong performance
across latency, energy, and scalability metrics, several limi-
tations indicate directions for improvement.
Simulation-Based Evaluation. All experiments were con-
ducted in EdgeSimPy [ 10], which models latency, work-
load variation, and migration dynamics but does not capture
hardware-level effects like scheduling delays or interference.
Physical 6G edge testbed deployment is needed to validate
robustness under real hardware constraints and user mobility.
Scalability V eriﬁcation. While simulations demonstrate
scalability up to 10,000 nodes, empirical validation on dis-
tributed edge testbeds with geographically separated clusters,

heterogeneous hardware, and varying link quality is required
to conﬁrm performance under realistic deployment constraints
beyond controlled simulation environments.
Semantic Adaptation. The 16-dimensional continual learn-
ing encoder is adaptive during training, but advanced online or
meta-learning strategies could improve adaptation to evolving
service semantics and unseen task categories.
Energy Modeling and Fault Tolerance. Power consump-
tion is estimated using utilization-based analytical models with
simulated DVFS and thermal throttling. While providing reli-
able comparative analysis (36.2 × improvement), future work
should incorporate hardware-in-the-loop proﬁling with phys-
ical RAPL sensors for absolute measurement precision. The
evaluation does not test multi-node failures or network parti-
tions; resilience-aware reward shaping and proactive failover
mechanisms are needed.
Baseline Diversity. Comparisons with four baselines
(FlatFedPPO, HierFedPPO, HSQF, RandomPlacement) could
expand to include centralized RL methods (SAC, TD3). Adap-
tive hyperparameter tuning could enhance performance across
network scales.
Addressing these aspects will advance FedSemGNN toward
a deployable orchestration system for real 6G edge environ-
ments.
