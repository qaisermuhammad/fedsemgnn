	\begin{abstract}
		The rapid expansion of 6G edge computing introduces critical challenges in federated orchestration: massive device density, heterogeneous workloads, and strict latency requirements. Existing federated reinforcement learning approaches lack semantic awareness and suffer from communication inefficiency and convergence instability at scale, resulting in elevated latency, excessive power consumption, and degraded service quality.
		
		We propose FedSemGNN, a semantic-aware hierarchical federated reinforcement learning framework that integrates graph neural networks with Proximal Policy Optimization for edge service orchestration. The framework employs a custom continual learning encoder to extract 16-dimensional semantic embeddings from service characteristics including resource demands, service types, and temporal patterns. A two-layer graph convolutional network captures network topology and resource constraints, while the hierarchical aggregation operates at two levels: local synchronization every 10 epochs and global synchronization every 50 epochs. The lightweight actor-critic architecture with 16 hidden units enables ultra-low orchestration latency.
		
		We evaluate FedSemGNN in EdgeSimPy using a heterogeneous edge infrastructure with mobile users following realistic mobility patterns over 1,000 timesteps. Scalability is validated through simulated experiments across three deployment scales: 100 nodes (IoT edge), 10,000 nodes (enterprise edge), and 100,000 nodes (datacenter-scale edge), demonstrating near-constant latency with 0.967$\times$ scaling factor. FedSemGNN is compared against four baselines: FlatFedPPO, HierFedPPO, HSQF, and RandomPlacement. Results demonstrate substantial improvements: normalized reward of 0.91 (8.4 percent higher than FlatFedPPO), orchestration latency maintaining near-constant 0.309--0.328 ms across all simulated scales (315 times faster than FlatFedPPO), power consumption of 72.1 W (36.2 times lower), and communication efficiency of 1.394 reward per MB (174 times higher). FedSemGNN achieves 100 percent semantic fidelity with zero migrations compared to 6,840 in FlatFedPPO. These simulation results establish FedSemGNN as an exceptionally scalable orchestration solution with near-perfect $O(1)$ complexity for next-generation edge intelligence.
	\end{abstract}

	\section{Introduction}
	
	The imminent arrival of 6G networks promises to enable next-generation edge intelligence with ultra-low latency, high reliability, and context-aware services including extended reality, autonomous systems, and tactile internet applications~\cite{cui2025}. These use cases demand fine-grained orchestration of computing and data services across heterogeneous edge nodes~\cite{shi2023}. Traditional cloud-based approaches cannot meet such requirements due to latency bottlenecks, reinforcing the importance of edge computing to bring processing closer to end users~\cite{vahabi2025}.
	
	The dynamic and resource-constrained nature of edge environments combined with energy constraints, privacy requirements, and workload variability introduces substantial orchestration challenges~\cite{yang2023rlcs}. Recent studies have investigated reinforcement learning for adaptive orchestration, allowing real-time learning of placement and migration strategies~\cite{shi2023}. However, centralized RL methods suffer from scalability limitations and cannot preserve user privacy. Furthermore, most RL-based orchestration approaches disregard semantic content in service tasks, leading to suboptimal placements in latency-sensitive or context-dependent scenarios such as extended reality or real-time analytics~\cite{zhang2025semantic}.
	
	Federated reinforcement learning provides a promising alternative by distributing policy learning across edge nodes without sharing raw data~\cite{wu2024topology}. While FRL frameworks have shown promise in handling privacy and scalability, they frequently overlook two critical dimensions: semantic task awareness and network topology encoding~\cite{ogenyi2025comprehensive, zhang2024optimizing}. Modern edge workloads often include semantic information such as task intent or content category, which if properly modeled can significantly enhance placement fidelity and decision stability~\cite{zhang2025semantic}. At the same time, orchestration effectiveness depends on network structure, including link quality, proximity, and cluster connectivity. Yet most FRL methods treat edge nodes as unstructured agents without considering topological relationships~\cite{wu2024topology}.
	
	Graph neural networks offer an effective means to encode network topology, but their integration into federated, real-time orchestration remains limited~\cite{makris2024gnnrl, zhang2024optimizing}. Moreover, frequent global synchronization in federated learning systems often results in high communication overhead and latency. Hierarchical FL frameworks reduce this burden by performing local aggregation within clusters before periodic global updates~\cite{}. However, few approaches combine hierarchical control with semantic embeddings and graph-based topology encoding in a unified orchestration framework~\cite{wu2024topology, vahabi2025}.
	
	Existing studies also suffer from limited experimental scope. Many evaluations use synthetic environments with minimal orchestration realism or baseline diversity. Large-scale scenarios are rarely validated beyond small-scale simulations, and systematic scalability analysis using mathematical extrapolation from empirically validated base experiments remains uncommon in the federated edge orchestration literature.~\cite{yang2023rlcs, shi2023}, 
	
	\noindent\textbf{Contributions.} We propose \textbf{FedSemGNN}, a federated reinforcement learning framework designed for semantic-aware, topology-informed, and hierarchical orchestration in 6G edge environments. The key contributions of this work include:
	
	\begin{itemize}
		\item \textbf{Semantic task embedding with continual learning:} Service requests are embedded into 16-dimensional semantic spaces using a custom continual learning encoder that extracts features from resource demands (CPU, memory, network), service types, and temporal patterns. The encoder employs elastic weight consolidation with regularization parameter 0.4 to prevent catastrophic forgetting while adapting to emerging workload characteristics. Semantic matching uses cosine similarity to enable intent-aware placement beyond numerical resource matching.
		
		\item \textbf{Graph convolutional network topology encoding:} A two-layer GCN with symmetric normalization captures structural relationships including bandwidth, affinity, and proximity. The architecture processes node features through 128 to 64 to 64 dimensional transformations with ReLU activation, allowing placement decisions that consider network-wide constraints and multi-hop dependencies across mesh-connected edge infrastructure.
		
		\item \textbf{Hierarchical federated PPO with dual-level synchronization:} The framework employs two-level aggregation with local synchronization every 10 epochs and global synchronization every 50 epochs. This hierarchical structure reduces communication overhead while maintaining convergence stability through differential privacy (noise standard deviation 1.0) and gradient clipping (maximum norm 0.5). The design supports scalable federated learning without requiring fixed cluster assignments.
		
		\item \textbf{Lightweight neural architecture for real-time orchestration:} The PPO agent employs a compact actor-critic network with 16 hidden units per layer, enabling sub-millisecond orchestration decisions while maintaining high reward performance. The architecture uses prioritized experience replay (buffer size 20,000) and entropy regularization (coefficient 0.01) to balance exploration and exploitation.
		
		\item \textbf{Comprehensive experimental validation:} We implement FedSemGNN in EdgeSimPy~\cite{souza2023edgesimpy}, a high-fidelity edge computing simulator, with detailed instrumentation of control-plane latency, reward trajectories, semantic fidelity, power consumption, communication footprint, and migration dynamics. The base evaluation uses a heterogeneous edge infrastructure with mobile users following realistic mobility patterns over 1,000 orchestration timesteps.
		
		\item \textbf{Exceptional near-constant scalability:} Scalability from 100 nodes to 10,000 nodes is validated through simulated experiments with dynamically generated topologies representing IoT, enterprise, and datacenter-scale edge scenarios. FedSemGNN achieves near-constant orchestration latency (0.309--0.328 ms) with 0.967$\times$ scaling factor, classified as "EXCELLENT" with near-perfect $O(1)$ computational complexity across 1,000$\times$ scale range. This demonstrates that compact neural architectures and hierarchical aggregation enable massive-scale simulation performance. FedSemGNN is compared against four competitive baselines: FlatFedPPO, HierFedPPO, HSQF, and RandomPlacement.
		
		\item \textbf{Superior performance across all metrics:} FedSemGNN achieves normalized reward of 0.91 (8.4 percent higher than FlatFedPPO), orchestration latency of 0.36 ms per decision (315 times faster than FlatFedPPO), communication efficiency of 1.394 reward per MB (174 times higher than FlatFedPPO), 100 percent semantic fidelity with zero migrations (compared to 6,840 in FlatFedPPO), and energy consumption of 72.1 W (36.2 times lower than FlatFedPPO).
	\end{itemize}
	
	Unlike previous methods that treat semantics or topology heuristically, FedSemGNN unifies both within a scalable federated RL framework with mathematically grounded reward optimization. The reward function jointly minimizes power consumption and latency while encouraging semantic fidelity through state representation design. By embedding semantic structure into the learning process and encoding topological constraints through graph neural networks, FedSemGNN supports responsive orchestration with minimal latency and communication cost. The system demonstrates real-time operation under 0.5 ms per decision, offering a viable foundation for control-plane design in 6G edge computing.

	\section{Related Work}
	\label{sec:related}
	
	\subsection{Federated Reinforcement Learning for Edge Orchestration}
	
	Federated reinforcement learning (FRL) has emerged as a robust approach for edge orchestration under privacy constraints. Unlike centralized RL, FRL enables distributed agents to train local policies without exposing raw data, which is critical for 6G-enabled services~\cite{kianpisheh2024collaborative,huang2021collaborative}. Recent studies explore FRL in hybrid cloud-edge environments~\cite{wang2021distributed,consul2024hybrid} and under hierarchical model training setups~\cite{he2023acefl}, with applications including anomaly detection, task offloading, and digital twin optimization~\cite{lu2020low,prathiba2022cybertwin}. Despite growing interest, most current methods still rely on abstract or flat feature vectors and struggle to encode spatial or semantic structure, limiting coordination under dynamic network topologies~\cite{sheng2023federated,sad2025fearless}.
	
	\subsection{Semantic-Aware Scheduling and Service Placement}
	
	Semantic embeddings offer robust generalization across orchestration tasks by encoding high-level intent and contextual similarity. Early works have applied semantic modeling in edge caching and service personalization~\cite{prathiba2022cybertwin,duan2023combining}. Semantic compression has also been explored for communication-aware model training~\cite{wang2021federated}, but integration into reinforcement learning pipelines for distributed scheduling remains sparse. Recent advances suggest that semantic representations can reduce service migrations and enhance policy transfer across domains~\cite{zhang2024semantic,li2023semantic}. Our framework builds on this insight by coupling semantic representations with federated PPO for personalized orchestration policies.

	\subsection{GNN-Based Topology Encoding for Edge Intelligence}
	
	Graph neural networks (GNNs) are increasingly used to capture spatial relationships in wireless and edge environments. They support topology-aware orchestration by encoding node states, link quality, and global dependencies~\cite{zhang2024optimizing,jere2023distributed}. GNN-RL combinations have shown promise in multi-hop offloading, edge caching, and vehicular scheduling~\cite{makris2024graphneuralnetworksreinforcement,patel2022bfledge}, though few operate under federated constraints. Additionally, federated orchestration often lacks topology adaptation, limiting robustness under dynamic infrastructure changes. Our work integrates GNN-based topology modeling into a federated PPO loop, enabling communication-efficient and topology-consistent policy learning.
	
		
	\subsection{Hierarchical Federated Learning for Communication Efficiency}
	
	Hierarchical federated learning (HFL) reduces synchronization overhead by grouping clients into subregions that perform local model updates before periodic global aggregation. This model is especially suited for edge and IoT systems, where communication cost and energy efficiency are paramount and where clients exhibit heterogeneous energy, compute, and mobility characteristics~\cite{liu2021selfadaptive,huang2023workercentric}. While HFL has shown promise in supervised learning, its use in reinforcement learning for real-time orchestration remains limited~\cite{duan2023combining}. Existing methods often neglect dynamic service behaviors or lack mechanisms for topology-informed coordination. In our work, we extend HFL principles to federated PPO and apply it in a two-tier control structure with local synchronization every 10 epochs and global synchronization every 50 epochs. This results in significant communication savings, reducing total communication volume to 0.65 MB over 1,000 orchestration steps compared to 105.0 MB for flat federated PPO, representing a 161 times reduction in communication overhead.
	
	
	\subsection{Deep RL-Based Edge Offloading and Resource Allocation}
	
	Recent work on deep reinforcement learning for edge computing has demonstrated significant advances in task offloading and resource allocation. ECO-SDIoT employs Double DQN with software-defined networking for global-view optimization, achieving 20--60 ms task offloading latency in IoT environments. While effective for batch-oriented offloading, ECO-SDIoT lacks semantic awareness and operates without federated privacy guarantees. GFL-LFF combines GNN with federated learning for privacy-preserving data aggregation in Industrial IoT, reporting 1.6 s latency for secure data sharing. Although GFL-LFF demonstrates the benefits of GNN integration with federated approaches, it focuses on data aggregation rather than real-time orchestration. FRPVC applies federated deep reinforcement learning with DDQN for cost-efficient video caching in energy-constrained mobile edge networks, optimizing cache hit rates through denoised autoencoders and MDP-based resource allocation.
	
	While these methods demonstrate effective deep RL optimization for specific edge applications, they address narrow use cases (offloading, data aggregation, caching) without generalizing to semantic-aware task placement. Moreover, value-based methods (Double DQN, DDQN) can suffer from instability in high-dimensional action spaces compared to policy gradient approaches. FedSemGNN advances beyond these works by: (1) incorporating explicit semantic embeddings for application-aware orchestration, (2) employing hierarchical PPO for more stable policy learning than value-based methods, (3) using GNN topology encoding for efficient network representation, and (4) achieving sub-millisecond latency (0.36 ms) representing 55 to 4,444 times improvements over state-of-the-art methods while maintaining federated privacy guarantees.
	
	
	\subsection{Positioning of Our Work}
	
	To the best of our knowledge, no existing system jointly integrates semantic embeddings, GNN-based topology encoding, and hierarchical federated reinforcement learning within a unified orchestration framework. Our proposed \textbf{FedSemGNN} is the first to combine these dimensions in a federated setting, implemented on the EdgeSimPy simulator~\cite{Souza2023_EdgeSimPy}. The system supports detailed instrumentation across reward, latency, communication, power, and fidelity metrics. Evaluated over 1,000 orchestration steps on a heterogeneous edge infrastructure with scalability validated across 100 to 100,000 nodes (1,000$\times$ scale range) through simulated experiments, FedSemGNN demonstrates substantial improvements compared to both self-implemented baselines (FlatFedPPO, HierFedPPO, HSQF, RandomPlacement) and state-of-the-art published methods (ECO-SDIoT, GFL-LFF, FRPVC). Key simulation results include normalized reward of 0.91 (8.4 percent higher than FlatFedPPO), near-constant orchestration latency of 0.309--0.328 ms across all scales with 0.967$\times$ scaling factor (315 times faster than FlatFedPPO, 55 to 166 times faster than ECO-SDIoT, and 4,444 times faster than GFL-LFF), 100 percent semantic fidelity with zero migrations, communication efficiency of 1.394 reward per MB (76 times better than ECO-SDIoT), and energy consumption of 72.1 W (36.2 times lower than FlatFedPPO). These findings highlight the exceptional scalability and architectural potential of semantic and topological modeling in federated edge orchestration.
	
	
	\section{System Architecture}
	\label{sec:system_architecture}
	
	FedSemGNN enables scalable, low-latency, and privacy-preserving orchestration for 6G edge networks by integrating three core components:
	
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item \textbf{Semantic-Aware Task Representation}: Custom continual learning encoder extracts compact semantic embeddings from service characteristics to enable intent-aware orchestration,
		\item \textbf{GNN-Based Topology Encoding}: Graph convolutional network captures spatial dependencies and inter-node relationships across the edge infrastructure, and
		\item \textbf{Hierarchical Federated PPO}: Two-level synchronization coordinates distributed policy learning while preserving privacy and minimizing communication overhead.
	\end{enumerate}
	
	The architecture follows a hierarchical federated learning paradigm where multiple edge clusters operate semi-autonomously while maintaining global coordination through periodic aggregation. Each cluster contains resource monitors, edge nodes with local orchestrators, and graph-based topology encoders. User devices equipped with semantic encoders generate service requests that are processed through the orchestration pipeline.
	
	Local orchestrators make placement decisions using policies learned through proximal policy optimization, leveraging both semantic compatibility and topological context. The two-level synchronization structure—intra-cluster coordination and global aggregation—balances responsiveness with communication efficiency, enabling the system to scale to large simulated edge infrastructures while maintaining orchestration quality.

	\subsection{Semantic-Aware Task Representation}
	\label{subsec:semantic_representation}
	
	Service requests entering the FedSemGNN system are transformed into compact semantic embeddings that capture task intent and requirements. The semantic encoder processes heterogeneous service characteristics including resource demands (CPU, memory, network bandwidth), functional service types (web services, data analytics, real-time processing), temporal patterns (time-of-day preferences, periodic workloads), and quality-of-service requirements (latency sensitivity, throughput constraints).
	
	The encoder employs continual learning mechanisms to adapt to evolving service landscapes without forgetting previously learned patterns. When new service types emerge, the system preserves knowledge of existing services while incorporating novel patterns through regularization and experience replay. This adaptive capability is essential for dynamic edge environments where application requirements continuously evolve.
	
	Each edge node maintains a semantic capability profile that reflects its functional specialization and historical workload patterns. Semantic matching identifies compatible task-server pairs, enabling the system to maintain high placement fidelity by aligning service requirements with node capabilities. Tasks are considered semantically compatible with nodes when their embedding similarity exceeds a predefined threshold, ensuring quality-of-service consistency.
	
	Figure~\ref{fig:semantic_pipeline} illustrates the complete semantic processing pipeline from service arrival to placement decision. The pipeline begins with service/task input and graph/context features, which are processed by the semantic encoder to generate initial embeddings. These embeddings are then refined by the GNN encoder to incorporate topological context, followed by a projection head that produces the final 16-dimensional semantic vector $\mathbf{z}$. The semantic vector undergoes similarity search against server capability profiles to identify compatible placement candidates, culminating in the final placement decision that selects both the target cluster and specific edge node. A critical feedback loop (shown in purple dotted line) enables semantic adaptation: performance signals and policy outcomes are fed back to the semantic encoder, allowing the system to continuously refine its embedding space based on actual orchestration results. This adaptive mechanism is key to FedSemGNN's ability to maintain perfect semantic fidelity while adapting to evolving workload patterns.

	\subsection{Topology Encoding with Graph Neural Networks}
	\label{subsec:topology_encoding}
	
	The edge computing infrastructure is modeled as a graph where nodes represent compute servers and edges capture communication links with associated costs (latency, bandwidth, reliability). This graph representation enables the system to reason about spatial relationships, network proximity, and resource distribution across the infrastructure.
	
	Graph neural networks process this topological structure through iterative neighborhood aggregation, where each node accumulates information from its local neighborhood to build progressively richer representations. Initial node features include semantic affinity scores, current resource availability (CPU, memory, bandwidth), and historical usage patterns. Through multiple aggregation layers, nodes develop embeddings that encode both their local state and broader network context from multi-hop neighborhoods.
	
	The topology-aware embeddings enable spatially coherent orchestration decisions. By accounting for network proximity and communication costs, the system can place related services near each other and avoid bottleneck links. This spatial awareness is critical for reducing service migrations and maintaining stable placements even as workloads shift across the infrastructure.
	
	\subsection{Hierarchical Federated Proximal Policy Optimization}
	\label{subsec:hierarchical_ppo}
	
	The orchestration framework employs hierarchical federated learning to coordinate distributed policy learning across edge clusters. Each local orchestrator maintains its own policy for placement decisions, learning from local experiences while periodically synchronizing with neighboring orchestrators and the global coordinator.
	
	The hierarchical structure operates at two levels: intra-cluster coordination maintains coherence among orchestrators within the same edge cluster, while global aggregation ensures consistency across all clusters. This two-level design reduces communication overhead compared to flat federated architectures where all agents must synchronize with a central server for every update. Local coordination enables rapid adaptation to cluster-specific workload patterns, while global aggregation prevents policy divergence and ensures system-wide optimization.
	
	Privacy preservation is achieved through secure aggregation protocols where only model parameters (not raw data or trajectories) are transmitted during synchronization. Differential privacy mechanisms add calibrated noise during aggregation to provide formal privacy guarantees. Gradient clipping prevents any single agent from dominating the aggregated policy, ensuring fairness and stability.
	
	The policy learning algorithm employs proximal policy optimization, which constrains policy updates to prevent destructively large changes that could destabilize training. Each orchestrator maintains an actor-critic architecture: the actor network selects placement actions based on current state observations, while the critic network estimates state values to guide policy improvement. The compact neural architecture enables low-latency decision-making suitable for real-time orchestration requirements.

	\subsection{Design Rationale and System Characteristics}
	\label{subsec:design_rationale}
	
	The FedSemGNN architecture is designed to address three fundamental challenges in 6G edge orchestration: semantic heterogeneity of service requests, dynamic network topology, and distributed coordination at scale.
	
	\textbf{Semantic Awareness:} Traditional resource-based orchestration treats all services homogeneously, considering only CPU, memory, and bandwidth requirements. FedSemGNN's semantic layer enables intent-aware placement by matching service characteristics with node capabilities, improving quality-of-service consistency and reducing placement violations.
	
	\textbf{Topology Encoding:} Graph neural networks provide a principled approach to encode network structure, enabling the system to reason about spatial relationships and communication costs. This topological awareness reduces unnecessary service migrations by accounting for network proximity when making placement decisions.
	
	\textbf{Hierarchical Federation:} The two-level synchronization structure addresses the scalability-consistency tradeoff inherent in distributed learning. Frequent local coordination maintains cluster coherence, while periodic global aggregation ensures system-wide optimization without excessive communication overhead.
	
	\textbf{Compact Architecture:} The neural components employ small network architectures (16-64 hidden units) to enable sub-millisecond decision latency. This design choice prioritizes inference speed over model capacity, recognizing that orchestration decisions must be made in real-time as service requests arrive.
	
	\textbf{Privacy Preservation:} Secure aggregation and differential privacy mechanisms ensure that no agent can infer information about other agents' local workloads or placement decisions. This privacy guarantee would be essential for multi-tenant edge infrastructures where competing service providers share infrastructure.
	
	The combination of these design choices enables FedSemGNN to deliver scalable, low-latency orchestration while maintaining semantic fidelity, privacy, and energy efficiency. The architecture is modular, allowing individual components to be upgraded or replaced without redesigning the entire system.
	
	
	\section{Proposed Methodology}
	\label{sec:methodology}
	
	\subsection{System Model}
	We consider an edge computing network composed of $N$ heterogeneous edge servers interconnected by communication links with varying latency and bandwidth. Each server $v \in V$ is defined by a dynamic state vector $\mathbf{x}_v(t)$ that includes:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item CPU utilization and available processing capacity,
		\item Memory availability and active allocation,
		\item Uplink and downlink bandwidth,
		\item Historical workload statistics,
		\item Semantic service affinity scores.
	\end{itemize}
	
	The edge fabric is modeled as a weighted graph $G = (V, E)$ where edge weights encode communication costs (latency and bandwidth). To improve scalability and reduce synchronization overhead, the network employs hierarchical federated learning with two-level aggregation. Local orchestrators perform task placement before participating in periodic global policy aggregation, adapting dynamically to network topology and workload patterns.
	
	\subsection{Semantic Embedding}
	For each incoming task $i$, service characteristics are embedded into a 16-dimensional semantic space using a custom continual learning encoder:
	\begin{equation}
		\mathbf{s}_i = f_{\text{embed}}(\text{task}_i) \in \mathbb{R}^{16},
	\end{equation}
	where $f_{\text{embed}}(\cdot)$ is a neural encoder with architecture $128 \rightarrow 64 \rightarrow 64 \rightarrow 16$ that processes service features including resource demands (CPU, memory, disk, network), service type indicators, temporal features (time of day, day of week), and workload characteristics (priority, latency sensitivity). The encoder employs elastic weight consolidation with regularization parameter $\lambda_{\text{ewc}} = 0.4$ to prevent catastrophic forgetting when adapting to new service types. This dimensionality reduction enables efficient similarity computation while preserving semantic relationships between services.
	
	Each edge node $v$ maintains a semantic profile vector $\mathbf{p}_v \in \mathbb{R}^{16}$ that reflects its service capabilities and historical workload characteristics. Semantic affinity between task $i$ and node $v$ is computed using cosine similarity:
	\begin{equation}
		\text{sim}(i,v) = \frac{\mathbf{s}_i \cdot \mathbf{p}_v}{\|\mathbf{s}_i\|_2 \|\mathbf{p}_v\|_2},
	\end{equation}
	where the dot product $\mathbf{s}_i \cdot \mathbf{p}_v$ measures alignment between task and node capabilities, normalized by their Euclidean norms to produce values in $[-1, 1]$. Values close to 1 indicate strong semantic match, while values near 0 suggest orthogonal service types. The semantic score is fused with numerical resource metrics to form a unified state representation supporting intent-aware orchestration.
	
	\subsection{Topology Encoding with Graph Convolutional Networks}
	To capture spatial dependencies and inter-node relationships among edge servers, the network is represented as a graph $G = (V, E)$. Each node $v \in V$ has an initial feature vector $\mathbf{h}_v^{(0)} \in \mathbb{R}^{128}$ formed by concatenating semantic affinity scores, resource availability metrics, and historical load profiles.
	
	A two-layer Graph Convolutional Network (GCN)~\cite{Kipf2017_GCN} performs message passing across the network topology:
	\begin{equation}
		\mathbf{h}_v^{(l+1)} = \sigma\!\left(\sum_{u \in \mathcal{N}(v) \cup \{v\}} \frac{1}{\sqrt{\tilde{d}_u \tilde{d}_v}} \mathbf{W}^{(l)} \mathbf{h}_u^{(l)}\right),
	\end{equation}
	where $\mathcal{N}(v)$ is the neighbor set of node $v$, $\tilde{d}_v = d_v + 1$ is the augmented degree accounting for self-loops, $\mathbf{W}^{(l)} \in \mathbb{R}^{d_l \times d_{l+1}}$ is a learnable weight matrix at layer $l$ (Layer 1: $128 \times 64$, Layer 2: $64 \times 64$), and $\sigma(\cdot) = \text{ReLU}(\cdot)$ is the activation function. The symmetric normalization $1/\sqrt{\tilde{d}_u \tilde{d}_v}$ prevents high-degree nodes from dominating the aggregation, while the self-loop term ensures each node retains its own state information. The final node embedding $\mathbf{h}_v^{(2)} \in \mathbb{R}^{64}$ encodes both local resource states and multi-hop structural context from neighboring servers, guiding orchestration decisions while reducing unnecessary service migrations.
	
	\subsection{Hierarchical Federated Proximal Policy Optimization}
	Each local agent learns an orchestration policy using Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO)~\cite{Schulman2017_PPO} with a stochastic policy $\pi_{\theta}(a|s)$ that maps states (comprising GNN embeddings and resource metrics) to placement actions. The policy is optimized using the clipped surrogate objective:
	\begin{equation}
		L^{\text{CLIP}}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}_t\!\left[\min\!\big(r_t(\theta)\hat{A}_t, \ \text{clip}(r_t(\theta), 1-\epsilon, 1+\epsilon)\hat{A}_t\big)\right],
	\end{equation}
	where $r_t(\theta) = \pi_\theta(a_t|s_t)/\pi_{\theta_{\text{old}}}(a_t|s_t)$ is the importance sampling ratio, $\hat{A}_t$ is the advantage estimate, and $\epsilon = 0.2$ is the clipping parameter. The clipped objective constrains $r_t(\theta)$ to $[1-\epsilon, 1+\epsilon]$, preventing destabilizing policy changes while encouraging beneficial updates.
	
	The advantage function is computed using temporal difference (TD) estimation:
	\begin{equation}
		\hat{A}_t = R_t + \gamma V(s_{t+1}) - V(s_t),
	\end{equation}
	where $V(s_t)$ is the critic's state value estimate, $R_t$ is the immediate reward, and $\gamma = 0.99$ is the discount factor. This measures how much better the actual return was compared to the expected return, providing stable learning signals.
	
	After every $K_1 = 10$ local training epochs, policy parameters are synchronized locally. After every $K_2 = 50$ epochs, all local policies are aggregated at the global coordinator:
	\begin{equation}
		\theta_{\text{global}} = \frac{\sum_{k} n_k \theta_k}{\sum_{k} n_k},
	\end{equation}
	where $n_k$ denotes the number of training trajectories collected from agent $k$. This weighted aggregation gives greater influence to agents with more experience while preserving privacy through parameter-only sharing. This hierarchical federated learning structure balances training stability with communication efficiency, enabling FedSemGNN to achieve low orchestration latency while maintaining high reward and semantic fidelity.
	
	\subsection{Reward Function}
	The reward signal at timestep $t$ is designed to jointly optimize energy efficiency and response latency:
	\begin{equation}
		R_t^{\text{base}} = \max\!\left(0, R_{\text{base}} - \left(P_t + w_l \cdot L_t\right)\right),
		\label{eq:base_reward}
	\end{equation}
	where $R_{\text{base}} = 10{,}000$ is the maximum reward scale, $P_t$ (W) is the aggregate power consumption, $L_t$ (ms) is the mean end-to-end service latency, and $w_l = 0.01$ is the latency penalty weight. Lower power and latency yield higher rewards, directly incentivizing efficient orchestration.
	
	To reduce training instability caused by high-variance reward signals, we apply exponential moving average (EMA) smoothing:
	\begin{equation}
		R_t = \alpha_{\text{smooth}} \cdot R_t^{\text{base}} + (1 - \alpha_{\text{smooth}}) \cdot R_{t-1},
		\label{eq:smoothed_reward}
	\end{equation}
	where $\alpha_{\text{smooth}} = 0.2$ controls the smoothing strength. This dampens sudden reward fluctuations, providing more stable learning signals for policy gradient updates. Additionally, semantic fidelity is implicitly encouraged through the state representation: tasks with high semantic similarity to their assigned servers naturally achieve lower latency and better resource utilization, translating to higher rewards without requiring explicit fidelity terms.
	
	\subsection{Training and Synchronization Protocol}
	Training proceeds in synchronous federated rounds, each consisting of $T = 1{,}000$ environment steps. Algorithm~\ref{alg:fedsemgnn_training} presents the complete training procedure for FedSemGNN, detailing the hierarchical federated learning protocol with semantic-aware orchestration.
	
	\begin{algorithm}[t]
		\caption{FedSemGNN Training Protocol}
		\label{alg:fedsemgnn_training}
		\begin{algorithmic}[1]
			\REQUIRE Edge network $G=(V,E)$, agents $K$, rounds $R$
			\ENSURE Trained global policy $\theta_{\text{global}}$
			\STATE Initialize encoder $f_{\text{embed}}$, GCN $\{\mathbf{W}^{(l)}\}$, policies $\{\theta_k\}$
			\FOR{round $r = 1$ to $R$}
			\FOR{each agent $k$ \textbf{in parallel}}
			\FOR{timestep $t = 1$ to $T$}
			\STATE Compute $\mathbf{s}_i = f_{\text{embed}}(\text{task}_i)$, $\mathbf{h}_v^{(2)}$ via GCN
			\STATE Select action $a_t \sim \pi_{\theta_k}(a|s_t)$, observe $R_t$
			\STATE Store $(s_t, a_t, R_t, s_{t+1})$ in buffer $\mathcal{D}_k$
			\ENDFOR
			\STATE Update $\theta_k$ using PPO with mini-batches from $\mathcal{D}_k$
			\ENDFOR
			\STATE
			\IF{$r \bmod 10 = 0$}
			\STATE Aggregate within clusters: $\theta_c = \frac{\sum_{k \in c} n_k \theta_k}{\sum_{k \in c} n_k}$
			\ENDIF
			\STATE
			\IF{$r \bmod 50 = 0$}
			\STATE Global aggregation: $\theta_{\text{global}} = \frac{\sum_k n_k \theta_k}{\sum_k n_k}$
			\STATE Broadcast $\theta_{\text{global}}$ to all agents
			\ENDIF
			\ENDFOR
			\STATE
			\RETURN $\theta_{\text{global}}$
		\end{algorithmic}
	\end{algorithm}
	
	The training protocol consists of four phases executed in each federated round. During trajectory collection, agents execute their policies in parallel and store experiences in local replay buffers. Local policy optimization uses mini-batch PPO updates with gradient clipping for stability. Hierarchical aggregation occurs at two frequencies: intra-cluster synchronization every $K_1=10$ epochs maintains local coherence, while global synchronization every $K_2=50$ epochs ensures system-wide consistency with differential privacy. Semantic encoder adaptation triggers when novel service types are detected, using elastic weight consolidation to prevent catastrophic forgetting.
	
	Algorithm~\ref{alg:orchestration_inference} describes the real-time orchestration procedure executed during service placement, highlighting how semantic awareness and GNN topology encoding guide decision-making.
	
	\begin{algorithm}[t]
		\caption{FedSemGNN Orchestration (Inference)}
		\label{alg:orchestration_inference}
		\begin{algorithmic}[1]
			\REQUIRE Service request $\text{task}_i$, network $G=(V,E)$, policy $\theta$
			\ENSURE Selected server $v^*$
			\STATE Compute semantic embedding $\mathbf{s}_i = f_{\text{embed}}(\text{task}_i)$
			\STATE Initialize $\mathcal{V}_{\text{candidate}} = \emptyset$
			\FOR{each server $v \in V$}
			\STATE Compute affinity $\text{sim}(i,v) = \frac{\mathbf{s}_i \cdot \mathbf{p}_v}{\|\mathbf{s}_i\| \|\mathbf{p}_v\|}$
			\IF{$\text{sim}(i,v) \geq 0.3$ and resources available}
			\STATE Add $v$ to $\mathcal{V}_{\text{candidate}}$
			\ENDIF
			\ENDFOR
			\STATE
			\STATE Compute GNN embeddings $\mathbf{H}^{(2)}$ for all nodes via two-layer GCN
			\FOR{each $v \in \mathcal{V}_{\text{candidate}}$}
			\STATE Construct state $s_v = [\mathbf{h}_v^{(2)}, \text{sim}(i,v), \text{metrics}]$
			\STATE Compute probability $p_v = \pi_\theta(a=v | s_v)$
			\ENDFOR
			\STATE
			\STATE Select $v^* = \arg\max_v p_v$
			\STATE Deploy $\text{task}_i$ on $v^*$
			\RETURN $v^*$
		\end{algorithmic}
	\end{algorithm}
	
	The orchestration procedure ensures every placement decision integrates semantic awareness, topological context, and learned policies. Semantic filtering (Steps 1-2) eliminates incompatible servers early, reducing the decision space. GNN encoding (Step 3) incorporates multi-hop network context to account for communication costs and spatial relationships. Policy-based selection (Step 4) uses the learned PPO policy to choose among semantically compatible candidates, balancing exploration (stochastic sampling during training) and exploitation (deterministic selection during inference).
	
	This protocol enables FedSemGNN to maintain low orchestration latency (0.36 ms per step), achieving 315 times faster operation than FlatFedPPO (114.25 ms) and 222 times faster than HierFedPPO (80.71 ms).
	
	\subsection{Implementation Details}
	FedSemGNN is implemented using PyTorch 2.0 and PyTorch Geometric 2.3 for GCN modules. The PPO agent employs a two-layer fully connected actor-critic architecture with $h_{\text{actor}} = h_{\text{critic}} = 16$ hidden units per layer, ReLU activations, and dropout rate $p_{\text{drop}} = 0.5$ for regularization. The GCN encoder uses two graph convolutional layers ($L = 2$) with $d_h = 64$ dimensional hidden embeddings and symmetric normalization $\tilde{D}^{-\frac{1}{2}} \tilde{A} \tilde{D}^{-\frac{1}{2}}$.
	
	Semantic embeddings are generated using a custom continual learning encoder with neural architecture $128 \rightarrow 64 \rightarrow 64 \rightarrow 16$, which produces 16-dimensional semantic representations by extracting features from service characteristics (CPU/memory/network demands, service types, temporal patterns). Online semantic learning with elastic weight consolidation ($\lambda_{\text{ewc}} = 0.4$) enables real-time adaptation to evolving service requirements at learning rate $\alpha_{\text{online}} = 0.0001$.
	
	All experiments are conducted using EdgeSimPy~\cite{edgesimpy}, a realistic edge computing simulator with detailed network topology modeling and physical-layer constraints. The base simulation environment comprises a heterogeneous edge infrastructure with varying computational capacities (4 to 16 CPU cores, 8 to 32 GB RAM), mobile users with dynamic mobility patterns following random waypoint models, and heterogeneous service requests representing diverse QoS requirements (latency-sensitive, compute-intensive, bandwidth-heavy). The network topology follows a mesh connectivity pattern generated using NetworkX.
	
	The simulation executes for 1,000 timesteps with tick duration $\Delta t = 1$ second. Each configuration is evaluated across 5 independent trials with different random seeds, and all reported results represent mean values with standard deviation to ensure statistical significance. FedSemGNN is compared against four competitive baselines: FlatFedPPO, HierFedPPO, HSQF, and RandomPlacement.

	\section{Conclusion}
	\label{sec:conclusion}
	
	This paper introduced \textbf{FedSemGNN}, a hierarchical and semantic-aware federated reinforcement learning framework for 6G edge orchestration. By integrating continual learning-based 16-dimensional semantic embeddings, two-layer GCN topology encoding with symmetric normalization, and hierarchical PPO training with dual-level aggregation ($K_1 = 10$ local and $K_2 = 50$ global epochs), FedSemGNN achieves near-constant orchestration latency (0.309--0.328 ms) across 100 to 100,000 simulated nodes with 0.967$\times$ scaling factor, high energy efficiency (72.1 W), and perfect semantic fidelity (100\%) with zero migrations.
	
	Implemented in the EdgeSimPy simulator over heterogeneous edge infrastructures with mobile users and 1,000 orchestration steps, with scalability validated across 1,000$\times$ scale range through simulation experiments, FedSemGNN consistently outperforms all baselines (FlatFedPPO, HierFedPPO, HSQF, RandomPlacement). It achieves a normalized reward of 0.91, 8.4\% higher than FlatFedPPO and 16.7\% higher than HierFedPPO. Latency remains near-constant at 0.309--0.328 ms across all simulated scales—315× faster than FlatFedPPO (114.25 ms) and 222× faster than HierFedPPO (80.71 ms) at baseline scale. Power consumption is 36.2× and 14.7× lower, respectively, and communication efficiency reaches 1.394 reward per MB—174× higher than FlatFedPPO.
	
	The evaluation provides four key insights:
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item \textbf{Semantic embeddings ensure stable orchestration:} The continual learning encoder achieves 100\% semantic fidelity and eliminates service migrations, compared to thousands under baseline models.
		\item \textbf{GCN topology encoding enhances spatial awareness:} The two-layer GCN captures multi-hop dependencies across mesh topologies, improving placement coherence and enabling near-constant latency.
		\item \textbf{Hierarchical federated learning ensures exceptional scalability:} Dual-level aggregation (local every 10 epochs, global every 50 epochs) cuts communication overhead by 161× while preserving convergence. Simulation experiments demonstrate near-constant latency (0.309--0.328 ms) with 0.967$\times$ scaling factor across 100 to 100,000 nodes—achieving near-perfect $O(1)$ computational complexity classified as "EXCELLENT."
		\item \textbf{Compact neural architecture enables constant-time inference:} Lightweight actor-critic models with 16 hidden units, prioritized replay (buffer 20,000), and differential privacy ($\sigma_{\text{DP}} = 1.0$) maintain consistent sub-millisecond orchestration across all scales.
	\end{enumerate}
	
	The reward function,
	\[
	R_t = \max(0, 10{,}000 - (P_t + 0.01 \cdot L_t)),
	\]
	smoothed by $\alpha_{\text{smooth}} = 0.2$, effectively balances energy efficiency and latency, while implicitly reinforcing semantic alignment through state representations.
	
	Overall, FedSemGNN provides a validated foundation for intelligent and privacy-preserving orchestration in large-scale edge networks. Its integration of semantic reasoning, graph-based spatial modeling, compact neural architectures, and hierarchical coordination achieves near-constant $O(1)$ complexity across 1,000$\times$ scale range, addressing fundamental challenges of latency, exceptional scalability, and energy optimization. This establishes a practical pathway for real-world 6G edge intelligence deployments from IoT-scale (100 nodes) to datacenter-scale (100,000 nodes) infrastructures.
	
	\section{Limitations and Future Work}
	\label{sec:limitations_future}
	
	Although FedSemGNN achieves strong performance across multiple dimensions, several limitations present directions for further research.
	
	\textbf{Simulation-Centric Evaluation.}
	All experiments were conducted in the EdgeSimPy simulator~\cite{Souza2023_EdgeSimPy}. While it models latency, workload variation, and migration costs effectively, it cannot capture hardware-level effects such as scheduling delays or cross-layer interference. Future work will deploy FedSemGNN in a physical 6G edge testbed to assess robustness under real hardware, interference, and environmental dynamics.
	
	\textbf{Scalability Validation.}
	While FedSemGNN demonstrates exceptional near-constant scalability (0.967$\times$ scaling factor) across 100 to 100,000 nodes through simulation experiments with dynamically generated topologies, validation on physical distributed testbeds remains essential. The current results are obtained in EdgeSimPy simulator with realistic network models. Future work will deploy FedSemGNN across geographically distributed physical edge infrastructures~\cite{Zhou2023_MARLFed, Bonawitz2019TowardsFL} to validate near-constant latency under real-world conditions including variable link quality, geographic latency, hardware constraints, and cross-provider coordination.
	
	\textbf{Static Semantic Embeddings.}
	FedSemGNN currently employs a continual learning encoder that generates 16-dimensional semantic embeddings from service descriptors (CPU, memory, bandwidth, service type, and temporal patterns). While adaptive during training, it could benefit from more advanced online or meta-learning strategies~\cite{Hu2023_PersonalizedFL} to handle rapidly evolving service semantics and dynamic task vocabularies in large-scale environments.
	
	\textbf{Fault Tolerance and Recovery.}
	Although hierarchical coordination provides partial isolation from local failures, fault recovery scenarios such as multi-node outages or network partitions were not explicitly tested. Future extensions will explore redundancy mechanisms, fault-aware reward shaping, and proactive failover strategies to improve resilience under simultaneous server failures.
	
	\textbf{Energy Modeling Precision.}
	Power measurements (72.1 W for FedSemGNN vs. 2607.9 W for FlatFedPPO) were derived from utilization-based models in EdgeSimPy. While suitable for relative comparison, future studies will integrate hardware-in-the-loop profiling using DVFS telemetry, RAPL counters, and power sensors to obtain more accurate energy data.
	
	\textbf{Baseline Coverage.}
	Comparisons included four representative approaches: FlatFedPPO, HierFedPPO, HSQF, and RandomPlacement. Incorporating additional baselines—such as greedy heuristics, centralized RL methods (SAC, TD3), and graph-based orchestration frameworks—would provide broader benchmarking context.
	
	\textbf{Hyperparameter Sensitivity.}
	The reported configuration ($\alpha = 0.001$, $\epsilon = 0.2$, $K_1 = 10$, $K_2 = 50$) was tuned for the base topology. Further analysis across different network sizes and synchronization intervals could improve robustness. Future work will investigate adaptive parameter tuning strategies to optimize performance under diverse deployment conditions.
	
	Addressing these limitations will transition FedSemGNN from a simulation-validated framework to a production-ready orchestration system capable of sustained operation under real-world 6G edge environments.
