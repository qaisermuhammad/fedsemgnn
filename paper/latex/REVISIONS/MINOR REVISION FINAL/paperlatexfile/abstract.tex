\begin{abstract}
6G edge orchestration must place heterogeneous services under strict latency, energy, and privacy constraints at massive device density. We present FedSemGNN, a semantic-aware hierarchical federated reinforcement learning framework that integrates continual semantic task embeddings, graph convolutional topology encoding, and two-level federated PPO synchronization to reduce communication overhead while preserving convergence stability.

FedSemGNN exposes tunable semantic matching ($\tau$) and continual-learning regularization ($\lambda_{\mathrm{EWC}}$) and supports priority-aware scheduling via latency weighting and priority-adaptive semantic thresholds for URLLC-style services. Implemented in EdgeSimPy, FedSemGNN is evaluated over 1,000 orchestration timesteps and compared with FlatFedPPO, HierFedPPO, HSQF, RandomPlacement, and a centralized PPO baseline. Results show improved reward and semantic fidelity with sub-millisecond decision latency, reduced migrations, and low communication footprint while scaling to 10,000 nodes with near-constant control-plane latency.
\end{abstract}
