\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper presented \textbf{FedSemGNN}, a hierarchical, semantic-aware federated reinforcement learning framework designed for 6G edge orchestration. By jointly leveraging Sentence-BERT semantic embeddings in 128-dimensional spaces, two-layer GCN-based topology encoding with symmetric normalization, and hierarchical PPO training with dual-level aggregation ($K_1 = 10$ epochs intra-cluster, $K_2 = 50$ epochs inter-cluster), FedSemGNN achieves ultra-low latency (0.36 ms), exceptional energy efficiency (72.1 W), and perfect semantic fidelity (100\%) with zero service migrations.

Implemented within the \emph{EdgeSimPy} simulator across 6 heterogeneous edge servers and 500 mobile users over 500 orchestration steps, and benchmarked against seven competitive baselines (FlatFedPPO, HierFedPPO, HSQF, Random, GreedyPower, GreedyLatency, FirstFit), FedSemGNN demonstrated exceptional performance across all metrics. The framework achieves normalized reward of 0.91 (2.9$\times$ higher than Random, 1.9$\times$ higher than GreedyPower), orchestration latency 315$\times$ faster than FlatFedPPO and 222$\times$ faster than HierFedPPO, power consumption 36.2$\times$ lower than FlatFedPPO and 14.7$\times$ lower than HierFedPPO, and communication efficiency of 1.394 reward per MB—174$\times$ better than FlatFedPPO.

The evaluation yielded four key insights:  
\begin{enumerate}
	\item \textbf{Semantic embeddings enable perfect placement stability}: Sentence-BERT (\texttt{all-MiniLM-L6-v2}) embeddings with cosine similarity matching achieve 100\% semantic fidelity with zero migrations, compared to 6,840 migrations in FlatFedPPO and 1,181 in HierFedPPO,
	
	\item \textbf{GCN topology encoding provides spatial coherence}: Two-layer graph convolutional networks with cached normalization capture multi-hop dependencies across mesh-connected infrastructure, enabling spatially aware orchestration decisions,
	
	\item \textbf{Hierarchical federated learning enables extreme scalability}: Dual-level aggregation (intra-cluster every 10 epochs, inter-cluster every 50 epochs) reduces communication overhead by 161$\times$ while maintaining convergence quality, with mathematical extrapolation demonstrating logarithmic scaling to 10,000+ nodes,
	
	\item \textbf{Compact neural architectures enable real-time operation}: Lightweight actor-critic networks with only 16 hidden units per layer, combined with prioritized experience replay (buffer size 20,000) and differential privacy ($\sigma_{\text{DP}} = 1.0$), achieve sub-millisecond orchestration latency suitable for 6G control-plane requirements.
\end{enumerate}

The reward function $R_t = \max(0, 10{,}000 - (P_t + 0.01 \cdot L_t))$ with exponential moving average smoothing ($\alpha_{\text{smooth}} = 0.2$) successfully balances energy efficiency and response latency while implicitly encouraging semantic fidelity through state representation design.

Collectively, these findings establish FedSemGNN as a principled, empirically validated foundation for intelligent, privacy-preserving orchestration in next-generation 6G edge computing systems. The framework's combination of semantic awareness, graph-based spatial reasoning, hierarchical coordination, and compact neural architectures addresses critical challenges in scalability, latency, energy efficiency, and semantic coherence—positioning it as a viable solution for real-world edge-AI deployments in emerging 6G networks.
